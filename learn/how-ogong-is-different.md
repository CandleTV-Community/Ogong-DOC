# How Ogong is different

Most networks that call themselves private or decentralized AI ask you to trust something: a company's promise not to log your prompts, a closed proxy you can't inspect, or a quality score that a good-enough cheaper model can quietly game.

Ogong replaces each of those with something you can check yourself. Your prompt is sealed to the provider, and the relay in between runs attested code you can inspect before trusting it. On the TEE tier you confirm the machine is blind to your prompt rather than taking anyone's word for it. And on nearly every request, the network proves the model you paid for is the one that actually ran, checking the computation itself rather than grading how nice the answer reads. What that adds up to is a guarantee that rests on math rather than on someone's word.
